# Brand design psychology guide

Use file for fast brand research. Match audience + promise + price point to visual choices.

## Typography

- Serif
  - meaning: tradition, authority, heritage, luxury
  - audience: older, professional, high-income
  - good for: finance, law, editorial, luxury
- Sans-serif
  - meaning: modern, clean, approachable, tech-forward
  - audience: broad consumer, millennials, Gen Z
  - good for: SaaS, startups, D2C, product brands
- Slab serif
  - meaning: bold, strong, rugged, confident
  - audience: niche enthusiasts, outdoor, male-skewed
  - good for: sports, automotive, construction, outdoor gear
- Script / handwritten
  - meaning: elegant, personal, artisanal, expressive
  - audience: boutique, food, wedding, craft
  - good for: bakeries, fashion, photography, handmade goods

## Color

### Single colors

- Red: urgency, passion, appetite, excitement. Good for youth, deals, food.
- Blue: trust, calm, security, corporate stability. Good for B2B, finance, privacy, older audience.
- Green: health, nature, money, growth. Good for wellness, eco, finance.
- Yellow / orange: optimism, friendliness, affordability, caution. Good for family, budget, youth.
- Purple: luxury, imagination, spirituality. Good for creative or premium brands.
- Black / white: power, minimalism, sophistication, exclusivity. Good for premium and minimalist brands.

### Color combos

- Monochromatic: focused, harmonious, modern, premium. Good for tech and premium SaaS.
- Analogous: calm, natural, comfortable. Good for wellness, lifestyle, nature.
- Complementary: high-contrast, energetic, loud. Good for sports, entertainment, youth.

## Motion

- Linear
  - meaning: mechanical, robotic, direct
  - good for: utility tools, industrial software, raw data UX
- Ease-in / ease-out
  - meaning: natural, human, fluid, premium
  - good for: consumer apps, polished tech, lifestyle brands
- Spring / bounce
  - meaning: playful, forgiving, gamified
  - good for: kids, learning, games, habit products
- Snappy / fast
  - meaning: speed, urgency, efficiency, sharp modern feel
  - good for: productivity, trading, delivery, ops tools

## Layout

- Symmetrical / centered
  - meaning: stable, formal, elegant, trustworthy
  - audience: traditional, premium
  - good for: luxury, corporate, reports, formal brands
- Asymmetrical / broken grid
  - meaning: edgy, creative, dynamic, rebellious
  - audience: Gen Z, creatives, early adopters
  - good for: agencies, streetwear, indie, art brands
- Modular / blocky / bento
  - meaning: organized, dense, reliable, clear
  - audience: B2B, power users, tech-heavy users
  - good for: dashboards, news, enterprise SaaS
- High whitespace / minimal
  - meaning: calm, premium, focused, exclusive
  - audience: affluent, wellness, design-sensitive buyers
  - good for: luxury, beauty, architecture, premium products

## Symbols and imagery

- Geometric / abstract symbols
  - meaning: logic, scale, connectivity, modernism
  - good for: tech, infra, platforms, large corporate systems
- Organic / hand-drawn assets
  - meaning: human, approachable, artisanal, imperfect
  - good for: local, craft, organic, community brands
- Gritty / high-contrast photography
  - meaning: raw, powerful, athletic, intense
  - good for: sports, fitness, energy, outdoor
- Soft / warm / bright photography
  - meaning: safe, caring, nostalgic, optimistic
  - good for: healthcare, family, travel, pets
- Flat vector illustration
  - meaning: friendly, safe, frictionless, generic-friendly
  - good for: onboarding, HR, fintech, consumer SaaS

## Fast mapping rules

- Enterprise B2B: blue, sans-serif, modular grid, ease-in/out
- Speed product: snappy motion, bold contrast, tight layout
- Premium product: whitespace, monochrome or restrained palette, serif or refined sans-serif, centered layout
- Playful product: bounce motion, bright palette, rounded forms, friendly illustration
- Rugged product: slab serif, dark earthy palette, gritty imagery, strong contrast
- Artisanal product: script accents, warm colors, organic imagery, imperfect shapes

## Research questions

Ask 3 things:

1. Who user?
2. What emotional promise?
3. What price point?

Then map answers to:

- type
- color
- motion
- layout
- imagery

## One-line summary

Brand feel come from repeated signals. Pick signals that match audience, promise, price.
